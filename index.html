The shape of the plan

The plan starts with a 10,830 September baseline, layers in the full 9-initiative system, and uses two reads for September. The base checkpoint remains 11,241. With purchase-intent landing page expansion added, the September planning range extends to ~11,945–12,195. That still leaves room to close to 13,402, but the plan now includes the second conversion engine required to make the stretch goal credible.

What gets us to September

The checkpoint is reached through BAU organic trend + initiative lift. The committed September number is 11,241. With purchase-intent landing page expansion added as the 9th initiative, the September planning range moves to ~11,945–12,195. Within that combined lift, /our-menu is still the cleanest near-term non-SEO lever because it already has traffic and an active redesign path, while purchase-intent landing page expansion creates the second conversion engine needed to narrow the remaining gap to 13,402.

What unlocks the aggressive target

Purchase-intent landing page expansion is the clearest next lever to add. These are not blog posts. They are landing pages targeting bottom-funnel queries like “meal delivery for athletes,” “meal delivery for busy professionals,” and “best meal delivery for weight loss,” built to convert like /our-menu.

What still remains true about the blog

Blog remains a capture channel first. The expected outcome is ~1,000 leads/month by September, feeding a nurture engine worth ~120–240 trials/year. That matters to the plan, but it is not the lever that closes a final September gap. /our-menu does that.

Why our target is achievable — a distribution problem, not a ceiling

Our conservative target (1.4%) is already beaten on 6+ of our pages. Our ambitious target (2.26%) is already beaten by our top 3 pages. The 0.47% overall rate is a distribution and infrastructure problem — 276 low-intent pages drag our average down. The path to 12K/year is making our top 50 pages perform like our top 10. No new traffic required.

Device context: 74% mobile — exit-intent won't work

74% of our blog traffic is mobile web (some pages exceed 80%). Exit-intent overlays don't reliably fire on mobile browsers. All of our CTA infrastructure must be scroll-triggered (fire at 40–60% scroll depth) or in-content. This is a non-negotiable constraint on our CTA rollout design.
